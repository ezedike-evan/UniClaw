# Campus Map
_Last updated: 2026-04-17_
_Source: community knowledge / UNILAG layout_

## Overview
The Main Campus (Akoka) is bounded roughly by the Lagoon (south) and the Akoka/Abule Oja gates (north). It's walkable end-to-end in about 20–25 minutes.

## Main gates
- **Main Gate (Akoka Road)** — primary entry/exit, heavy traffic peak hours.
- **Second Gate** — towards Bariga.
- **University of Lagos Staff Quarters Gate** — usually restricted to staff/residents.

## Key landmarks
- **Senate Building** — central administrative building.
- **Main Library (Centre for Information Technology & Systems adjacent)** — the anchor of the central academic area.
- **J. F. Ade Ajayi Auditorium** — big events and graduations.
- **Main Auditorium** — smaller events, talks.
- **Tayo Aderinokun Lecture Theatre (TALT)** — popular large lecture hall.
- **The Lagoon Front** — relaxation spot on the southern edge.
- **Main Bowl (Sports Complex)** — sporting events, inter-faculty games.
- **UNILAG Guest Houses** — northern part of campus.

## Faculty zones (rough geography)
- Engineering & Environmental Sciences cluster near the Second Gate side.
- Science complex is central, near the Library.
- Arts and Social Sciences cluster around the central academic area.
- Management Sciences has its own block near the main road through campus.
- Medicine is at **Idi-Araba**, a separate campus across town.
- Law is split between locations. [VERIFY]

## Getting around
- Walking is the default for most students.
- **Shuttle buses** run regular routes around campus at a low flat fare. [VERIFY current fare]
- **Okada / keke** are restricted or banned in many zones — check current campus rules.
- Most students live in Akoka, Abule Oja, Bariga, and Yaba and commute by bus or keke to the gates.

## Parking
- Each faculty has a small staff car park.
- Visitor parking near the Senate Building and Main Library. [VERIFY]

## FAQs
Q: I'm lost on my first day — what should I do?
A: Head to the Senate Building or any faculty reception. Students and porters give directions freely. Landmarks to orient by: Senate Building, Main Library, J. F. Ade Ajayi Auditorium.

Q: Is the campus safe at night?
A: Most central areas are well-lit and patrolled, but avoid isolated routes late at night. Use the shuttle or walk in groups.
