# Exams
_Last updated: 2026-04-17_
_Source: UNILAG Academic Office / Student Portal_

## Overview
UNILAG runs two semesters per session. Each semester ends with a formal examination period. Timetables are issued by the Academic Office and faculty examination officers.

## Exam types
- **Continuous Assessment (CA)** — tests, assignments, practicals, usually worth 30–40% of the final grade.
- **Semester examinations** — written exams at the end of each semester, worth the remaining 60–70%.
- **Resit / carryover exams** — for students who fail specific courses; scheduled at the start of the following session.
- **Supplementary exams** — rare, case-by-case for medical and other documented reasons.

## Where to find your timetable
1. Student Portal → Examinations tab.
2. Faculty notice board (physical).
3. Departmental WhatsApp/Telegram group (unofficial but fast).

Always cross-check at least two sources — errors and last-minute room changes are common.

## Grading scale (typical)
- A: 70–100 (5.0)
- B: 60–69 (4.0)
- C: 50–59 (3.0)
- D: 45–49 (2.0)
- E: 40–44 (1.0)
- F: 0–39 (0.0) [VERIFY — some faculties use slightly different bands]

## Results portal
Results are released on the Student Portal after Senate approval. Expect a lag of several weeks after the exam period.

## FAQs
Q: I think my result is wrong.
A: File a remark/script review request through your HOD within the window (usually 2 weeks after results are released). Pay the prescribed fee.

Q: I missed an exam because of illness.
A: Submit a medical report from the UNILAG Medical Centre to your HOD and faculty exam officer as soon as possible.

Q: I'm stressed about exams — any advice?
A: Easy. Sleep matters more than cramming — aim for 7+ hours. Past questions are gold; the library and senior students have them. Group study in short focused sessions. If the stress is overwhelming, the Counselling Centre is a real option.
